Field Operations
Experience Redesign

Redesigning a field operations platform to reduce friction, modernize the interface, and improve overall SaaS usability.

Role UX Research · UI/UX Design
Duration Ongoing

Overview

This project focused on redesigning a field operations platform used for:

  • agent visits,
  • indent management,
  • collections,
  • and retailer operations.

The redesign was not limited to optimizing workflows. It also focused on improving the overall product experience by reducing user friction, modernizing the interface, and introducing a cleaner, more minimal design that supports greater usability, product adoption, and scalable SaaS growth.

The challenge was to redesign the experience with minimal disruption to existing workflows while significantly improving usability and interface consistency.

The Problem

Over the years, the product evolved to support changing business needs, but the growing number of workflows and interactions made the experience less intuitive and harder to navigate.

Field executives had to move through multiple screens to complete routine tasks.

Existing Flow
Home → Actions → Agents → Agent Actions → Indent Edit → Dates → Records

Users also faced:

  • repeated back navigation,
  • excessive interaction steps,
  • manual date-by-date record searching,
  • disconnected operational tasks,
  • inconsistent UI patterns,
  • and inactive waiting during visit validation.

The interface lacked visual consistency and modern usability standards, creating friction during high-frequency field operations.

Key Insight

Field executives do not think in modules.

They think:

“I’m working with this agent.”

The experience needed to feel continuous, contextual, and operationally focused rather than separated into disconnected screens and actions.

At the same time, improving the visual experience and simplifying interactions became equally important for improving overall product perception and long-term SaaS scalability.

Design Approach
Friction Reduction Across Workflows
The redesign focused on identifying friction points across the product and simplifying repetitive operational flows without drastically changing the existing system structure. The goal was to improve usability while keeping the learning curve minimal for existing users.
Agent-Centric Workspace
Introduced a unified operational workspace containing: visit status, indent overview, operational actions, and activity records within one contextual screen. This reduced unnecessary navigation and improved workflow continuity.
Modern Minimal Interface
Redesigned the interface using a cleaner and more minimal visual approach focused on: improved hierarchy, simplified layouts, reduced visual clutter, consistent spacing, and clearer interaction patterns. The updated UI aimed to create a more modern SaaS experience while improving usability across operational tasks.
Background Visit Timer
The mandatory visit timer was redesigned to run in the background while users continued: collections, retailer onboarding, and indent operations. This converted waiting time into productive operational time.
Calendar-Based Record Visibility
Added: date indicators, activity previews, and quick record filtering to improve indent discoverability and reduce manual searching effort.
UX Principles Applied
Reduce Friction Workflow Continuity Reduce Cognitive Load Minimal Interface Design Recognition Over Recall Contextual Task Grouping Scalable SaaS UX
Expected Impact
  • Faster task completion
  • Reduced navigation effort
  • Improved workflow continuity
  • Cleaner and more modern user experience
  • Reduced inactive time during visits
  • Better usability across operational tasks
  • Improved product perception for SaaS adoption
Reflection

This project reinforced that improving enterprise products is not only about redesigning workflows, but also about reducing friction across the entire experience.

A modern, minimal, and operationally efficient interface can significantly improve usability, user confidence, and overall product value without requiring drastic workflow changes.
