Designing structured production workflows for inventory, grading, and dispatch operations.

Overview

This project focused on redesigning operational workflows used across production, grading, reconciliation, packaging, and dispatch processes within a manufacturing environment.

The goal was to improve operational visibility, reduce manual dependency, and create a more structured workflow system for production teams.

The Problem

Production operations relied heavily on:

  • paper-based tracking,
  • disconnected workflows,
  • manual inventory updates,
  • and inconsistent grading processes.

Teams lacked clear visibility into:

  • inventory movement,
  • batch tracking,
  • damaged stock,
  • and dispatch readiness.

This created:

  • reconciliation issues,
  • slower grading operations,
  • tracking inaccuracies,
  • and operational delays across production stages.
Users & Stakeholders

The platform was designed for multiple operational teams involved in the production lifecycle, including:

  • production supervisors,
  • grading operators,
  • inventory managers,
  • packaging teams,
  • and dispatch coordinators.

Each team required quick access to operational data, workflow visibility, and accurate inventory tracking to maintain production efficiency.

Core Workflow
Purchase Order → Grading In → Grading Out → Reconciliation → Packaging → Dispatch

The challenge was improving continuity and visibility across every stage of the workflow.

Design Approach
Structured Workflow System
Designed the experience around clear operational stages so users always understood: current workflow state, next action, and inventory movement. This improved operational clarity across teams.
Simplified Data Entry
Production environments require fast and accurate interactions. The redesign introduced: grouped forms, guided inputs, auto-filled fields, and simplified operational flows to reduce repetitive manual effort.
Inventory & Batch Visibility
Improved visibility into: available quantities, used quantities, batch information, and production tracking. This reduced allocation mistakes and improved reconciliation accuracy.
Damage Classification Workflow
Designed quick-selection panels for: broken, cracked, spoiled, and undersized inventory. This improved operational speed and standardized reporting during grading workflows.
Operational Dashboard Visibility
Added centralized visibility into: production flow, grading status, inventory movement, and dispatch readiness. This improved tracking and coordination across operational teams.
UX Principles Applied
Workflow-Based UX Operational Visibility Error Prevention Reduce Cognitive Load Structured Data Entry Process Continuity
Expected Impact
  • Faster grading workflows
  • Improved inventory accuracy
  • Reduced PO errors
  • Better reconciliation tracking
  • Improved production visibility
  • Faster operational coordination
Reflection

This project reinforced that operational products are heavily dependent on clarity and structure.

In production environments, every interaction affects:

  • speed,
  • accuracy,
  • and operational confidence.

The redesign focused less on visual complexity and more on creating workflows that helped teams work faster and make fewer mistakes.
