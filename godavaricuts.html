Overview

This project focused on designing a D2C mobile ordering experience for a retail brand transitioning from physical stores to digital commerce.

The primary goal was to preserve the trust, familiarity, and confidence of the offline shopping experience while creating a simple and modern mobile ordering flow.

The project focused heavily on:

  • brand identity,
  • checkout UX,
  • delivery scheduling,
  • and cart experience optimization.
The Problem

The target users were already familiar with the brand through physical stores.

The challenge was making the digital experience feel:

  • familiar,
  • trustworthy,
  • and operationally clear

instead of feeling like a generic e-commerce app.

Users needed confidence in:

  • product selection,
  • delivery timing,
  • address management,
  • pricing transparency,
  • and final payment calculations.
Design Approach
Brand-Led Interface
The visual system was built around the brand identity itself. Used: brand colors, category illustrations, localized visual cues, and familiar interaction patterns to make the product feel connected to the physical retail experience.
Simplified Product Browsing
Designed lightweight product browsing with: clean product cards, clear pricing, fast quantity interactions, and reduced visual clutter. The experience focused on helping users make faster purchase decisions.
Checkout & Cart Optimization
The cart experience became the primary UX focus. The redesign included: delivery slot selection, coupon interactions, wallet balance handling, bill transparency, and sticky payment actions. The goal was to reduce hesitation before payment.
Delivery Scheduling Experience
Designed simplified time-slot selection with: strong selection states, cleaner scheduling patterns, and clear operational visibility. This improved delivery confidence during checkout.
Delivery Partner Workflows
Also designed partner-side operational screens for: order overview, order details, and nearest-branch dispatch workflows. The experience focused on fast operational scanning and delivery coordination.
UX Principles Applied
Trust-Centered UX Reduce Checkout Friction Brand-Led Design Operational Clarity Progressive Disclosure Decision Simplicity
Expected Impact
  • Improved checkout confidence
  • Better alignment between offline and digital experience
  • Faster product selection
  • Improved payment clarity
  • Stronger brand perception
  • Better delivery coordination visibility
Reflection

This project reinforced how strongly trust influences commerce experiences.

For offline-first users, familiarity and clarity matter just as much as functionality.

Small interaction details during checkout can significantly affect confidence, conversion behavior, and overall product perception.
